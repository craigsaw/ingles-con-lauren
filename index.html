My Philosophy

As an English teacher, I have refined my teaching style by experimenting with different teaching methods and theories. This trial-and-error has provided me with many valuable insights into teaching, learning, and reaching goals.

When faced with challenges in the classroom, I rely on these common-sense principles as my guide:

  • - Have a specific plan
  • - Improve the plan when possible
  • - Personalize content for learner ease
  • - Have an inspiring classroom
  • - Be infectious with enthusiasm!
  • - Be encouraging and supportive
  • - Practice, practice, practice!

Have a specific plan.

It is important to set clear goals for what we want to achieve, to establish a clear timeframe for achieving it, and to build a good study plan to get us there. I begin formulating this plan during the first encounter with a new student and I track progress regularly, especially if there are specific deadlines to be met.

Make modifications when necessary.

Things don't always go as planned, so it is important to adapt to new circumstances and reconsider previously held strategies. If one thing doesn't work, try something else.

Everyone learns differently.

Each student is a unique bundle of personality, ability, drives, interests, goals, and learning styles. I try make classes fun, personal, and engaging by customizing them to each student. Learning is much easier when it's interesting!

Onda matters.

My office and classroom are filled with inspiring artwork, fresh flowers, and comfortable furnishings, because environment and vibe are important when we are stretching ourselves by trying to learn new things.

Get focused!

I begin classes with a 'clearing' of any mental obstacles or preoccupations that may keep a student being fully present and ready to learn. By quickly addressing these issues, we set them aside so we can learn and grow.

Enthusiasm is infectious!

I love language, and I love to share my love of language! I want my students to develop a deep appreciation for English and to gain the confidence necessary to express themselves in it.

Practice, practice, and more practice!

Learning a language doesn't only require practice, it IS a practice!! The more time you spend speaking English with native speakers, the better you will be at speaking English with native speakers!!
